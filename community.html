Culture & Language

Kalenjin Language School

Kalenjin Language School is an integral part of the Taunet Nelel community.

The Kalenjin people are an ethnic grouping of eleven culturally and linguistically related tribes: Kipsigis, Nandi, Keiyo, Marakwet, Sabaot, Pokots, Tugen, Terik, Sengwer, and Ogiek. Their homeland is Kenya's western highlands and the Rift Valley — spread across Nandi, Uasin Gishu, Kericho, Bomet, Elgeyo-Marakwet, Baringo, Trans Nzoia, Laikipia, and West Pokot. These speakers are distributed across different counties in Kenya, with many concentrated in the former Rift Valley province. Many speakers now live across Victoria, Australia.

Why are we teaching the Kalenjin language?

  • To preserve and promote cultural heritage and identity.
  • To strengthen intergenerational transmission of the language.
  • To give learners a sense of pride and belonging in their community.
  • To prevent language loss and ensure Kalenjin continues to thrive alongside global languages like English and Swahili.

Benefits of learning the Kalenjin language

  • Cultural identity: Deeper understanding of traditions, history, and values.
  • Communication: Better interaction with families, communities, and elders who prefer Kalenjin.
  • Cognitive benefits: Multilingualism enhances memory, creativity, and problem-solving.
  • Opportunities: Research, cultural tourism, storytelling, and local leadership roles.
  • Social cohesion: Unity and belonging within the Kalenjin community.

Long-term goals

  • Language revitalization and prevention of decline.
  • A generation of fluent Kalenjin speakers, readers, and writers.
  • Integration of Kalenjin into education, media, literature, and digital spaces.
  • Pride in indigenous languages while balancing global competitiveness.
  • Documentation and standardization for future generations.

Role of parents and community

  • Parents: Speak Kalenjin at home, encourage children to respond in the language, and use it in daily activities.
  • Community elders: Share oral traditions, stories, and proverbs to keep cultural wisdom alive.
  • Wider community: Create spaces — festivals, church, ceremonies, media — where Kalenjin is actively used.

How we deliver

  • Schools: Introduce Kalenjin lessons in the curriculum where possible; run weekend or evening classes.
  • Digital platforms: Apps, social media, and YouTube for wider reach.
  • Storytelling sessions: Oral traditions, songs, and folktales.
  • Print & media: Books, children's stories, newspapers, and community programs in Kalenjin.
  • Monthly literacy sessions through Taunet Nelel community programs.

Target groups

  • Children and youth — the main focus for sustaining the language.
  • Parents and guardians — key transmitters of language at home.
  • Teachers and educators — for formal Kalenjin classes and community programs; to maintain connection to roots.

Kalenjin Culture

Kalenjin Culture is an integral part of the Taunet Nelel community.

The Kalenjin have a vibrant cultural heritage that includes music, dance, and traditional ceremonies. They have unique rituals and practices associated with events like births, marriages, and funerals. Traditional music and dance are integral parts of their social gatherings.

Whether you're passionate about Kalenjin culture or eager to make new connections, you're warmly invited to join us — membership is open to all. Our focus is on supporting and empowering all our members.

  • Kalenjin Culture programs
  • Kalenjin Language School
  • Rites of passage ceremonies
  • Traditional marriage ceremonies
  • Biannual Kalenjin cultural education

Taunet Nelel Women

The Taunet Nelel Women group provides a safe space for women in Victoria to interact, reflect, support, educate, and encourage one another.

How we achieve our goals:

  • Advocate for the independence and freedom of women and girls.
  • Promote community cohesion and inclusion in Taunet Nelel.
  • Provide networking opportunities for girls and women.
  • Connect culturally diverse women with decision-makers.
  • Develop mentorship programs for women in leadership in Australia.
  • Devise policies that favour women and girls within our community.
  • Advocate for equal footing with male peers.
  • Implement community projects aligned with empowering women.

In Australia, migrant and multicultural women face challenges including job search, housing, culture shock, isolation, mental health, relationships, and finances. Taunet Nelel Women provides networking, shared experiences, and mentoring for work-related skills — through workshops, community engagement, and women's events.

Taunet Nelel Men's Affairs

Welcome to Taunet Nelel Men's Affairs — a safe, inclusive, and empowering space for men in our community.

Who are Taunet Men? Members of Taunet Nelel who come together for open dialogue, mutual support, and shared experiences that uplift and strengthen our community.

Why join? A community of men who support, encourage, and share freely — through activities that foster harmony, mental and emotional health, and strong masculine identity.

Why was it established? To address silent issues men face, particularly in our Kalenjin community. Social pressures often leave men suffering in silence — raising risks of mental health problems, loneliness, and unmet emotional needs. Taunet Nelel Men seeks to end that cycle through connection, dialogue, and community engagement — including our Men's Affairs campfire gatherings.

Taunet Mental Wellbeing Department

Mental wellbeing is a core part of every Taunet Nelel community member's health.

Mission: Culturally sensitive mental health guidance, support, and resources for the Kalenjin community in Melbourne — with special focus on international students.

Vision: A thriving, inclusive Kalenjin community where mental health awareness is promoted, stigma is reduced, and members access tools to enhance wellbeing.

Strategic objectives:

  • Promote mental health awareness through culturally appropriate campaigns and education.
  • Resource navigation — referrals, crisis helplines, and government programs.
  • Support for international students facing cultural adjustment and academic pressure.
  • Community engagement through group discussions and outreach.
  • Capacity building via a sustainable mental health committee.
  • Collaboration with Beyond Blue, Lifeline, Headspace, and multicultural services.

International students face higher rates of anxiety and depression. Language barriers and limited social support contribute to these outcomes — our department addresses this with culturally tailored workshops and elder involvement.

Useful resources (Victoria & Australia)

  1. Department of Health Victoria — Mental Health
  2. Mental Health Foundation Australia — Support Groups
  3. Black Dog Institute — Digital Tools
  4. Head to Health
  5. Lifeline — 24/7 crisis support
  6. Beyond Blue
  7. Victoria emergency mental health helplines

Reach out to the mental health committee or Taunet Nelel leadership for tailored support.

Taunet Nelel Welfare — Victoria

A compassionate community initiative supporting registered Kalenjin community members in Victoria with timely, respectful, and dignified financial support during bereavement.

Why Taunet Welfare? Losing a loved one is emotionally and financially overwhelming — especially far from home. Our Welfare eases immediate financial pressure and honours our culture of standing together in difficult moments.

Benefits of joining:

  • Bereavement financial support for members and immediate family (as defined in the constitution).
  • Dignified community response and solidarity in times of loss.
  • Peace of mind knowing support is available when needed.
  • Ongoing communication and guidance from the Welfare team.

How it is financed: One-time joining fee (AUD $300 within 90 days of registration), equal member contributions after disbursements, annual fundraising dinner (first weekend of July), and community donations.

Taunet Nelel Welfare isn't just a financial service — it's a community promise to honour our culture and protect our people.
